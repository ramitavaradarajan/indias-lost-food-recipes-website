Dessert Recipes

Kaju Katli

  • 1 cup cashews
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder

Steps:

  1. Grind cashews into a fine powder.
  2. In a pan, mix sugar and water, and heat until it forms a one-string consistency syrup.
  3. Add cashew powder to the syrup and stir continuously until it forms a smooth dough.
  4. Add ghee and cardamom powder, mix well, and remove from heat.
  5. Roll the dough into a thin layer, cut into diamond shapes, and let it cool before serving.

Rasgulla

  • 1 liter milk
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder

Steps:

  1. Boil the milk and add lemon juice or vinegar to curdle it.
  2. Strain the curdled milk using a muslin cloth to get chenna (paneer).
  3. Knead the chenna until smooth and divide it into small balls.
  4. Boil sugar and water in a wide pan, add the chenna balls, and cook until they double in size.
  5. Add cardamom powder, simmer for a few minutes, and let it cool before serving.

Rice Kheer

  • 1/2 cup basmati rice
  • 1 liter milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios)
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/4 tsp saffron strands (optional)

Steps:

  1. Rinse the basmati rice under water and soak it for 30 minutes.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring the milk to a boil.
  3. Add the soaked and drained rice to the boiling milk and simmer on low heat, stirring occasionally.
  4. Once the rice is cooked and the kheer has thickened, add sugar and mix well.
  5. Add chopped nuts, cardamom powder, and saffron strands (if using).
  6. Continue to cook until the kheer reaches your desired consistency.
  7. Remove from heat, let it cool, and refrigerate before serving.

Rasmalai

  • 1 liter milk
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios)
  • 1/4 cup saffron-infused milk (optional)

Steps:

  1. Boil the milk and add lemon juice or vinegar to curdle it.
  2. Strain the curdled milk using a muslin cloth to get chenna (paneer).
  3. Knead the chenna until smooth and divide it into small flat discs (malai).
  4. In a separate pan, prepare a sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water.
  5. Add the chenna discs to the sugar syrup and cook until they double in size.
  6. Add cardamom powder, chopped nuts, and saffron-infused milk (if using).
  7. Simmer for a few minutes, let it cool, and refrigerate before serving.

Kalakand

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios)
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • Saffron strands for garnish (optional)

Steps:

  1. Boil the milk and add lemon juice or vinegar to curdle it.
  2. Strain the curdled milk using a muslin cloth to get chenna (paneer).
  3. Transfer the chenna to a pan and cook on low heat, stirring continuously.
  4. Add sugar and continue to cook until the mixture thickens and leaves the sides of the pan.
  5. Add chopped nuts and cardamom powder, mix well.
  6. Transfer the mixture to a greased tray and flatten it evenly.
  7. Garnish with saffron strands (if using) and let it cool before cutting into pieces.
