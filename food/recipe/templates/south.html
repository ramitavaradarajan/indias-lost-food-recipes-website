South Indian Recipes

Dosa

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Rice
  • 1 cup Urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon Fenugreek seeds
  • Salt, to taste
  • Water, as needed

Steps:

  1. Wash and soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately for 6 hours.
  2. Grind rice and urad dal separately to a smooth batter.
  3. Mix the batters together, add salt, and let it ferment overnight.
  4. Heat a griddle, pour a ladle of batter, spread it in a circular motion, and cook until golden brown.
  5. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.

Idiappam (String Hoppers)

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups Rice flour
  • 1 cup Hot water
  • Salt, to taste

Steps:

  1. In a bowl, mix rice flour with hot water and salt to form a smooth, soft dough.
  2. Load the dough into an idiappam press and press out the dough onto idli plates in a circular motion.
  3. Steam the pressed dough for about 10-15 minutes until it's cooked.
  4. Serve idiappam hot with coconut milk or curry of your choice.

Upma

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Semolina (Rava)
  • 2 cups Water
  • 1 Onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup Mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans)
  • 2 Green chilies, chopped
  • 1/2 inch Ginger, grated
  • 1/4 cup Cashews
  • 1/2 teaspoon Mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon Urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon Chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • A pinch of Asafoetida (hing)
  • Curry leaves, a few
  • 2 tablespoons Ghee or oil
  • Salt, to taste

Steps:

  1. In a pan, heat ghee or oil. Add mustard seeds, urad dal, chana dal, and cashews. Sauté until they turn golden.
  2. Add asafoetida, chopped onions, green chilies, grated ginger, and curry leaves. Sauté until onions become translucent.
  3. Add mixed vegetables and sauté for a few minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, in a separate pan, roast semolina (rava) until it turns golden and gives a nice aroma.
  5. Pour water into the vegetable mixture and bring it to a boil. Add salt to taste.
  6. Slowly add the roasted semolina, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  7. Cook on low heat until the upma thickens and is fully cooked.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with chopped cilantro. Enjoy your South Indian Upma!

Inippu Paniyaram

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Idli/Dosa Batter
  • 1/2 cup Jaggery, grated
  • 1/4 cup Coconut, grated
  • 1/2 teaspoon Cardamom powder
  • A pinch of Baking soda
  • Ghee or oil for greasing

Steps:

  1. In a bowl, mix idli/dosa batter, grated jaggery, grated coconut, cardamom powder, and baking soda.
  2. Heat the paniyaram pan and add a few drops of ghee or oil in each mold.
  3. Pour the batter into the molds and cook until the edges turn golden brown.
  4. Flip the paniyarams and cook the other side until fully cooked.
  5. Remove from the pan and serve warm. Enjoy the sweet and flavorful Inippu Paniyaram!

Kolukattai

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Rice flour
  • 1/4 cup Split yellow moong dal
  • 1/4 cup Grated coconut
  • 1/4 cup Jaggery, grated
  • 1/4 teaspoon Cardamom powder
  • A pinch of Salt
  • Water, as needed
  • Banana leaves or oil for greasing

Steps:

  1. Soak split yellow moong dal in water for 1-2 hours.
  2. In a bowl, mix rice flour, soaked moong dal, grated coconut, jaggery, cardamom powder, and a pinch of salt.
  3. Add water gradually and knead the mixture into a smooth dough. The consistency should be such that it can be shaped easily.
  4. If using banana leaves, cut them into small squares and briefly pass them over an open flame to make them pliable.
  5. Take a small portion of the dough, shape it into a ball, and place it on the center of a banana leaf or greased surface.
  6. Press and flatten the ball into a circular shape, folding the edges of the leaf to encase the mixture.
  7. Repeat the process for the remaining dough portions.
  8. Steam the kolukattai for 15-20 minutes until they are cooked and firm.
  9. Allow them to cool slightly before serving. Enjoy your delicious Kolukattai!

Arusi Parupu (Dal Tadka)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 1 Onion, finely chopped
  • 2 Tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 Green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds
  • A pinch of Asafoetida (hing)
  • 2-3 Dry red chilies
  • A few Curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons Ghee or oil
  • Salt, to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, for garnish

Steps:

  1. Wash and pressure cook toor dal with turmeric powder until it is soft and cooked thoroughly.
  2. In a pan, heat ghee or oil. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, asafoetida, dry red chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté until mustard seeds splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until the tomatoes are soft and the oil starts to separate.
  5. Add the cooked toor dal to the pan. Mix well and bring it to a boil. Adjust the consistency by adding water if needed.
  6. Add salt to taste and simmer for a few minutes to let the flavors meld together.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  8. Your Arusi Parupu (Dal Tadka) is ready to be served. Enjoy it with rice or Indian bread!
